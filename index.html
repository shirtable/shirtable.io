Learn to program.

A low-stress coding workshop for non-techies

You are a successful and intelligent professional working in a non-technical field (marketing, policy, education, management). You know that software is the future and you need to learn more about computers and coding, but online coding classes are too impersonal, and coding bootcamps seem intimidating.

In our basics workshop you will learn the fundamentals of programming in Python, walking away with techniques for structuring and manipulating data to gain insights that would be tedious, time consuming, and often impossible from a spreadsheet.

Leveraging a data set containing more than 200,000 baby name entries from data.gov (yes, you read that right), you will write code that can answer questions such as, "how many male babies were born in 2012 with four-letter names?" and "how many babies were born in 2010?"

While the technical challenges you face in your career probably won't involve baby names (but really, you never know), the techniques you learn can be applied to any data set, allowing you to answer complex questions about your data in just a few commands.

In the basics workshop you will learn more than just the basics of the Python programming language -- you will learn how to think like a software developer so that you can effectively tackle technical challenges, communicate with technical teams and most importantly, understand how solutions to complex data problems are built from simple pieces which can be assembled with even just a basic knowledge of Python.

Why Python?

Python is a powerful yet easy to learn programming language that's used everywhere from web servers, to NASA, to Google's Compute Engine. Python is more than just a programming language. It's also a community that understands that code is more than just instructions for computers - it's for people, by people. We want to help get you plugged in to a community of folks that want you to succeed and grow.

We are Shirtable

Joshua R. Smith and Marty McGuire are Shirtable.

Joshua Smith's specialty is developing mathematical models of physical systems and implementing them in software using industry-standard software development practices. In his day job, Joshua is a Senior Computational Scientist at Xometry and is responsible for designing and implementing Xometry's geometric analysis and quoting engine. Joshua has over a decade of experience writing Python code.

Marty McGuire is a web and operations developer with 12+ years experience building and deploying websites for clients such as MakerBot and Adafruit Industries.

We take an empathetic but hands-on approach to teaching coding skills based on the most recent computing education research. The lessons are designed to be approachable by professionals not in a technical field without being patronizing -- you have worked hard to be successful in your career and are trying to learn new skills. We create a welcoming environment for you to learn, and we give you the opportunity to leverage our experience in web technologies and scientific computation. Participants are encouraged to help one another and apply what they have learned to their own research problems.
